Interpretation :

Euclidean distance will be a fundamental measure in the case of hierarchical clustering analysis on standardized measurements. After that, Agnes algorithm will be used to contrast the single, complete, average, and Ward linkage methods with a view to determining the best clustering method based on the output. Clustering stability can be checked by dividing data into partitions and comparing cluster assignments across partitions. Data normalization will be necessary in determining a cluster of healthy cereals for elementary school cafeterias based on the fact that all the cereals make a nutritional contribution toward students health.

Key findings from the analysis:

The optimal number of clusters identified is four. Cluster Structure: Cluster 1: Includes cereals that are highly nutritious, having a balanced distribution of fiber, protein, and low content of sugar/fat. Cluster 2: Includes cereals that have moderately well-rounded nutritional profiles, providing a balanced mix of nutrients. Cluster 3: Includes cereals whose sugar level is high and fiber level is low, suggesting lower nutritional value. Cluster 4: Grains that show generally lower nutritional value due to an increased level of sugar and fat.

Normalization of data is imperative for precise comparison between clusters. The recommended cluster grouping is Cluster 1, which includes cereals corresponding to IDs (69, 61, 27, 44, 58, 64, 2, 65, 66, 55, 58, 21, 35, 18, 31, and 67).
